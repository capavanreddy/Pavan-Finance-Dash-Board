# 📘 FinPulse Operational Bible

Welcome to the **FinPulse Operational Bible**. This document serves as the master guide for the Finance Hub, outlining the purpose, structure, and general workflows of the entire system.

---

## 1. Vision & Purpose
FinPulse is a centralized **Finance Team Task Manager** designed to eliminate operational silos, automate recurring financial activities, and provide a transparent audit trail for all departmental requests and payments.

### Core Objectives:
*   **Automation**: Move away from manual reminders and spreadsheets for recurring tasks.
*   **Accountability**: Clear ownership and review cycles for every activity.
*   **Visibility**: Real-time dashboards for pending actions, overdue tasks, and payment trackers.
*   **Access Control**: Granular permissions to ensure data security across different finance functions.

---

## 2. System Architecture (The "Four Pillars")

The system is built on four primary functional modules:

### Pillar 1: Task Dashboard (Workplace)
The engine room where all one-off and converted recurring tasks are managed.
*   **Ad-hoc Tasks**: Created manually for non-recurring work.
*   **Converted Tasks**: Automatically generated from templates.
*   **Lifecycle**: Pending -> Under Review -> Completed/Rejected.

### Pillar 2: Learning Opportunities (LO)
A continuous improvement tracker for errors and process refinements.
*   **Internal Learnings**: Track mistakes or gaps found during reviews.
*   **Reporting**: Isolate learnings by department or individual.
*   **Closure**: Ensures that every mistake leads to a documented "Learning Opportunity."

### Pillar 3: Recurring Activities & Daily Tasks
The automation hub for predictable financial cycles.
*   **Templates**: Define patterns (Monthly, Quarterly, etc.).
*   **Staging Area**: Preview tasks before they hit the live dashboard.
*   **Daily Tracker**: Specialized high-frequency tracking for items that must happen every single day.

### Pillar 4: Payments Calendar & Analytics
The financial commitment tracker.
*   **Tracker**: Real-time status of vendor payments, rent, and utilities.
*   **Master Sheet**: Central repository for all recurring payment obligations.
*   **Analytics**: Visual trends on payment timelines (Paid early vs. Late).

## 3. General Workflows

### A. The Request-to-Task Workflow
1.  **Request Entry**: A user from any department submits a request.
2.  **Allocation**: Authorized "Allocators" assign the request to the correct Finance owner.
3.  **Task Conversion**: Once allocated, the request becomes a "Task" in the dashboard.
4.  **Completion**: The Finance owner completes the task, and the original requester is notified.

### B. The Recurring Cycle
1.  **Rule Creation**: Admin sets a rule (e.g., "GST Filing - Monthly").
2.  **Staging**: System generates "Pending Conversions" for the upcoming month.
3.  **Approval**: Admin or Manager "converts" these into live tasks with assigned owners.

### C. The Review & Audit Cycle
*   Every task has an **Owner** and a **Reviewer**.
*   Ownership cannot be self-approved; a second pair of eyes is required for "Reviewer" status.
*   Audit logs track who created, moved, or deleted any record.
